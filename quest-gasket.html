UX Case Study

Quest Gasket

A complete redesign of Quest Gasket’s industrial website, including a new sitemap, e-commerce structure, and UX-focused product navigation system.

Project Details

Project Type
End-to-end Website + Branding
Role
Sole UX/UI Researcher & Designer
Industry
Industrial Sealing / Manufacturing
Tools
Balsamiq, Figma
View live website

Overview

Quest Gasket is an industrial sealing manufacturer that needed a complete website overhaul. Their existing site was outdated, difficult to navigate, and lacked a clear product structure. The client also wanted to introduce an online store to improve internal efficiency and reduce manual ordering.

I led this project end-to-end — from research and information architecture to wireframes and handoff — designing a modern, intuitive website aligned with current UX standards.

The Challenge

The original website had several issues:

  • Complex and confusing product organization
  • No clear hierarchy or pathways for finding products
  • No e-commerce system
  • Outdated navigation patterns and layout
  • Heavy reliance on manual quoting and email communication

The client wanted a website that:

  • Felt modern and professional
  • Made product discovery intuitive
  • Reduced staff workload through an online store
  • Reflected industrial conventions while remaining approachable for non-technical users

Project Goals

  • Create a clean, scalable information architecture for a large product catalog
  • Build a new sitemap to streamline navigation
  • Wireframe a full e-commerce flow
  • Clarify product specs, technical data, and ordering options
  • Provide an interactive prototype for client review before development

Process

1. Website Audit

I began with a full evaluation of the existing site, identifying redundant pages, inconsistent product groupings, missing content, and dead-end navigation paths. This audit became the foundation for restructuring the entire site.

2. Information Architecture & Sitemap

Working closely with the client, I reorganized the product catalog and created a completely new sitemap. We grouped related product categories, set clear naming conventions, and designed straightforward paths from homepage → category → product → checkout.

The sitemap went through client review sessions and was refined to support current needs and future product expansion.

3. Wireframing the New Website

Using Balsamiq, I created low-fidelity wireframes for the homepage, navigation, product category pages, product detail pages, technical information sections, a video library, and e-commerce flows.

The wireframes were hot-spotted to mimic interactions, allowing the client to click through and experience the flow before visual design and development.

4. Product Page System

Because Quest Gasket offers many product types, I designed a flexible layout system that could be reused across product pages while accommodating different technical needs.

The product page template included:

  • Key specs and technical data in a scannable layout
  • Technical drawings or diagrams
  • Downloadable PDFs or videos for installation and product details
  • Clear options for “Request a Quote” or “Add to Cart”

Outcome

All wireframes were presented to the client using a clickable prototype. After review and minor refinements, the design was approved and handed off to the development team with clear annotations and structure.

  • Created a scalable structure for a large and complex product catalog.
  • Introduced Quest Gasket’s first e-commerce-ready website framework.
  • Clarified product navigation and reduced redundancy in the information architecture.
  • Delivered an interactive prototype to support implementation and stakeholder alignment.

What I’d Do Next

  • Run usability testing with key customer segments post-launch.
  • Introduce advanced filtering and search for product specifications.
  • Explore product comparison functionality for similar SKUs.
  • Monitor analytics to refine navigation and content based on behavior.
