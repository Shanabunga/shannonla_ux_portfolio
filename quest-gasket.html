UX Case Study

Quest Gasket Website Redesign

A full website redesign for an industrial supplier, focused on creating a clear product hierarchy, modern navigation, and a scalable foundation for a future online store.

Project Details

Project Type
Website Redesign + Information Architecture
Role
UX Research, IA, UI (Lo-Fi)
Industry
Industrial Sealing / Manufacturing
Tools
Balsamiq, Figma
View live website

Overview

Quest Gasket is an industrial sealing manufacturer whose legacy website made it difficult for customers to find the correct product family, series, or technical information. The structure was flat, the navigation was unclear, and internal staff often had to guide buyers manually.

The company also wanted to introduce an online store—but the existing architecture couldn’t scale to support ecommerce. My goal was to modernize the site, reorganize the product structure, and design a sitemap flexible enough for current needs and future growth.

The Challenge

The original website suffered from major UX issues:

  • Unclear and inconsistent product categories
  • No hierarchy to guide users through families and series
  • No ecommerce system or supporting structure
  • Outdated navigation and terminology
  • High reliance on manual support and quoting

The biggest challenge:

Reorganizing the product catalog and creating an information architecture that could support a future online store—something the existing structure simply couldn’t handle.

Project Goals

  • Build a clean, scalable sitemap for a large product catalog
  • Modernize patterns to reflect current UX standards
  • Create a structure that supports both browsing and “I know what I need” flows
  • Prepare the site for ecommerce implementation
  • Deliver a clickable prototype to validate the flow before development

Process

1. Website Audit

I began by auditing the existing site. This included documenting navigation gaps, redundant pages, inconsistent naming, and unclear product groupings. The audit revealed that users had no clear path to move from category → product → specification.

2. Information Architecture & Sitemap

Working with the client, I created a new sitemap that introduced a clear hierarchy: product families → series → specifications/materials. This aligned with how customers actually search and how internal teams describe products.

The sitemap went through several iterations, ensuring it remained flexible enough to support the planned online store.

3. Wireframing the New Website

I produced low-fidelity wireframes using Balsamiq for the home page, navigation levels, product pages, technical information, and ecommerce paths. These wireframes were hot-spotted to create a clickable prototype that stakeholders could navigate.

4. Product Page System

Quest Gasket’s catalog includes many unique product types, so I designed a reusable, flexible system for product detail pages. Each template supports technical data, drawings, downloadable resources, and clear actions such as “Request a Quote” or “Add to Cart” (for future ecommerce).

Outcome

The final sitemap and wireframes were presented through a clickable prototype. After review and small refinements, the design was approved and handed off to development with clear documentation.

  • Created a scalable structure for a large and complex product catalog.
  • Introduced Quest Gasket’s first ecommerce-ready website framework.
  • Clarified navigation patterns and reduced redundancy across pages.
  • Delivered an interactive prototype to align stakeholders before build.

The redesigned website is now live: questgasket.com

What I’d Explore Next

  • Usability testing once the new structure has real user traffic
  • Advanced filtering and search for product specifications
  • Product comparison tools for similar SKUs
  • Analytics-driven improvements to navigation patterns
