UX Case Study

Flying Wings

Designing a white-label flight booking experience with a complete end-to-end flow that can be branded for multiple airline and travel partners.

Project Details

Project Type
End-to-End Website & White-Label Prototype
Role
Sole UX/UI Researcher & Designer
Industry
Travel & Tourism / SaaS
Tools
Figma, Balsamiq, Zoom

Overview

Flying Wings is a white-label travel booking platform created for a client based in China. The project was initiated through a Canadian partner who works primarily in the airline industry. They provided a high-level sitemap and needed a desktop flight booking experience that could be branded for their client and reused for future airline partners.

I designed both non-branded and branded high-fidelity prototypes in Figma, covering the full booking journey from search to checkout. The goal was to deliver a realistic, clickable experience that could be used in client presentations and as a foundation for development.

The Challenge

The team needed a booking flow that worked for two different purposes:

  • A realistic flight booking experience for travelers.
  • A clean, structured demo that sales could present to potential clients.

Starting from only a basic sitemap, the design had to:

  • Look modern and trustworthy for an international audience.
  • Be easy to rebrand for different airline and travel partners.
  • Show a complete journey from homepage to confirmation.
  • Be fully clickable so stakeholders could “feel” the experience.

Project Goals

  • Create an end-to-end flow (search → results → details → checkout → confirmation).
  • Support both non-branded and branded UI states.
  • Use patterns that are familiar from modern travel sites.
  • Design a layout that can be easily white-labeled for future clients.
  • Deliver a Figma prototype with hotspot interactions for demos and reviews.

Process

1. Client Sitemap & Structure

The client provided a simple sitemap with top-level navigation: Air, Hotels, Cars, About Us, Contact Us. From there, I expanded the structure into a complete flow that supported:

  • Homepage with hero area and destination specials.
  • Flight search module with trip type, cabin class, and passengers.
  • Search results with outbound and inbound options.
  • Trip summary and detailed pricing.
  • Checkout with passenger and billing information.

2. Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Using Balsamiq, I created low-fidelity wireframes to define layout and hierarchy before moving into visual design. The wireframes focused on:

  • A clear homepage with a search module and featured destinations.
  • Simple, scannable flight results with pricing and trip time details.
  • A checkout page that balances detail with clarity and trust.

3. Non-Branded High-Fidelity Prototype

Once the flow and layout were validated, I moved into a non-branded high-fidelity prototype. This version established:

  • Typography scale and visual hierarchy.
  • Form styles for the search and checkout steps.
  • Card-based layout for destination specials.
  • Consistent headers, footers, and navigation patterns.

4. Branded High-Fidelity Prototype

With the base experience in place, I created a branded version tailored to the client’s airline partner. This included:

  • Custom colors and brand typography.
  • Travel imagery reflecting key destinations.
  • Updated hero copy and call-to-action messaging.

5. Search, Results & Selection Flow

The core booking flow centered on making it easy to search, compare, and select flights. The results layout included:

  • Outbound and inbound flight lists with pricing.
  • Trip duration, layovers, and “Direct” labels.
  • Clear “Select”, “Book it”, and “Booked” states.
  • Consistent layout for both outbound and inbound segments.

6. Trip Summary & Checkout

After selecting flights, users see a combined trip summary and price breakdown, followed by passenger and billing forms. The checkout flow was designed to be familiar and trustworthy, with:

  • Clear outbound and inbound flight details.
  • Base price and taxes/fees separated.
  • Fields for passenger information and contact details.
  • Billing address and payment-related details.
  • Visible terms and conditions to build trust.

7. Interactive Prototype

To support remote reviews and client demos, I created a fully hot-spotted prototype in Figma. Stakeholders could click through:

  • Homepage → Search → Results.
  • Flight selection and trip summary.
  • Checkout and confirmation.

The prototype is currently used in beta for internal and client-facing presentations.

Outcome

The final deliverable included both non-branded and branded high-fidelity prototypes, along with a complete interactive flow from search to checkout. The client approved the design and it was handed off to the development team for implementation.

  • Delivered a full end-to-end booking journey in a clickable prototype.
  • Created a reusable white-label base that can be themed for future clients.
  • Provided a polished demo flow for sales presentations and onboarding.
  • Established clear patterns for search, results, selection, and checkout.

What I’d Do Next

  • Run usability testing with travelers to validate clarity and flow.
  • Layer in analytics to understand drop-off points along the journey.
  • Optimize layouts for mobile and tablet experiences.
  • Explore features such as fare alerts, saved trips, and loyalty integration.
